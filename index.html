Method

Two logics of value.

The work is built around two sponsorship value logics: exposure-driven value creation, where value comes from reach and visibility, and B2B relationship-driven value creation, where value comes from network and trust built over time. Very few partnerships are a pure example of one or the other — but knowing which logic is actually driving a given deal is the difference between selling signage and selling something a sponsor is genuinely looking for.

Proof

What the research found.

Independent research into Danish professional football (CBS thesis, 2026) found that:

  • Sponsorship value isn't created by exposure alone, but by the interplay of exposure, activation, relationships, and track record.
  • Visibility, attendance, and European participation are important revenue drivers — but explain only part of total sponsorship value.
  • Sponsors increasingly buy access to networks, hospitality, and decision-makers — not just logo placement.
  • A significant share of sponsorship value sits in assets most clubs still don't measure, document, or use systematically.
  • There is substantial untapped commercial potential in Danish football through more strategic use of data and value documentation.
  • Sponsorship deserves to be treated as a strategic leadership area, not a marketing or sales task, given it's one of clubs' most important revenue sources.
  • ESG and community engagement are shifting from support activities into genuine commercial assets, as sponsors increasingly look for partnerships with documentable social value.

This isn't theory — it was tested through an applied case analysis of Brøndby IF, identifying four concrete areas for commercial improvement, prioritized by expected revenue impact and implementation complexity. The same approach shapes the work with every new club. This thinking is also laid out in my op-ed for Idrætsmonitor.

Foundation

A Copenhagen Business School thesis, tested on Brøndby IF.

My starting point is a thesis from Copenhagen Business School, where — together with Victor Flavio Jelsbak — I investigated what actually drives sponsorship revenue in Danish professional football. We combined five years of financial data across five Superliga clubs with interviews of clubs, sponsors, and agencies, and found something worth paying attention to: exposure (attendance, broadcast reach, European participation) only explains part of the picture. The rest comes down to how well a club activates its rights, and how strong a business network it builds between its sponsors.

That insight is the foundation of Beyond the Logo. I work with clubs that want a clearer, evidence-based picture of where their commercial value actually sits — and a concrete plan for capturing it, whether that's in sponsorship, fan data, or matchday revenue.
